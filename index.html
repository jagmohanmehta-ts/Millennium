About — 02

A pharmaceutical distribution group, built quietly and at scale.

Millennium Group operates through its flagship company Millennium Medisolutions Private Limited — a Private Limited company incorporated in 2018 and headquartered in Gurugram, Haryana.

Pharmaceutical distribution is a business of unglamorous, day-after-day reliability. The group's purpose is to be exactly that, for the manufacturers we represent and the trade we serve.

The group was established in 2018 with the incorporation of Millennium Medisolutions Private Limited, registered with the Registrar of Companies, Delhi (CIN U74999HR2018PTC076964). Operations are run from the company's premises at Village Khandsa, off NH-8 near Hero Honda Chowk, Gurugram — a location chosen for its logistics reach into Haryana and the wider National Capital Region.

Today the business employs around 162 professionals across warehousing, fulfilment, accounts, sales and the administrative functions that keep a regulated distribution operation working. Through its trade and supply network, the group reaches pharmacies, hospitals and institutional buyers across the region.

Across both branded and generic medicine ranges, the group's standard is constant: stock that is authentic, correctly handled, properly documented, and delivered when it is needed.

01 Scope

This privacy policy explains how Millennium Medisolutions Private Limited (operating as Millennium Group, hereafter the company, we or our) handles personal and business information collected through trade interactions, telephone and email correspondence, and any digital channels operated by the group.

02 Information We Collect

We may collect: (a) identity and contact information such as name, designation, business name, address, telephone, email and statutory identifiers like GSTIN and drug-licence number; (b) commercial information including order history, invoices, payment references and delivery acknowledgements; and (c) communication records from calls and emails exchanged with us in the course of trade.

03 How We Use Information

Information is used to operate the distribution business — processing and fulfilling trade orders, raising GST-compliant invoices, verifying drug-licence eligibility, scheduling deliveries, maintaining trade accounts, responding to enquiries and protecting against fraud or product diversion.

04 Sharing & Disclosure

We share information only as needed: (a) with statutory and regulatory authorities where disclosure is required by law; (b) with logistics, transport and payment partners strictly to perform a requested service; and (c) with professional advisers under confidentiality obligations. We do not sell personal information.

05 Retention

We retain information for as long as needed to serve the purposes above and to meet record-keeping obligations under the Companies Act, the GST regime, the Drugs and Cosmetics Act and other applicable law. When no longer required, records are securely deleted or anonymised.

06 Security

We apply reasonable administrative, physical and organisational safeguards proportionate to the sensitivity of the information held, including controlled access to records and care in electronic communication. No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure, and we ask trade partners to apply equivalent care.

07 Your Choices

Trade partners may request access to, correction of, or deletion of personal information we hold, subject to identity verification and any overriding legal record-keeping obligation. Requests may be made in writing to our Gurugram office.

08 Updates & Governing Law

This policy may be updated from time to time; the current version is the one published here. It is governed by Indian law, and the courts at Gurugram, Haryana have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising in connection with it.

01 Definitions

'Company', 'Millennium Group' or 'Millennium' means Millennium Medisolutions Private Limited, the operating Private Limited company of the group, based in Gurugram, Haryana. 'Counterparty', 'you' or 'your' means any pharmacy, hospital, clinic or other person engaging with the company. 'Goods' means pharmaceutical products supplied by the company.

02 Trade Eligibility

Goods are supplied on a business-to-business basis only. Trade accounts and orders are open to entities holding a valid drug licence and other applicable statutory registrations. By placing an order, the counterparty confirms that such registrations are current and that the person placing it is authorised to do so.

03 Orders & Acceptance

An order placed by phone, email or other accepted channel is an offer to purchase. The company's acceptance occurs on confirmation or dispatch of the order; until then, the company may decline or partially fulfil an order, including where stock or licence verification is not satisfied.

04 Pricing & Taxes

Prices are those confirmed at the time of order acceptance and are exclusive of taxes unless stated otherwise. Applicable GST is charged on every invoice. Prices are subject to change for future orders in line with supplier pricing and market conditions.

05 Payment

Payment is due on the terms communicated at order acceptance — advance, on delivery, or on agreed credit. Amounts outstanding beyond agreed terms may lead to suspension of further supply and recovery action, and may attract interest where notified.

06 Delivery, Title & Risk

The company aims for timely delivery, but timelines are good-faith estimates rather than guarantees. Risk in the Goods passes on delivery to the counterparty or its nominated carrier; title passes only on receipt of full payment. The counterparty should inspect Goods on delivery and record any shortage or visible damage on the delivery document.

07 Handling & Resale of Goods

The counterparty undertakes to store, handle, dispense and resell the Goods in compliance with the Drugs and Cosmetics Act and applicable rules, to preserve storage conditions where required, and not to tamper with packaging, labelling or batch information.

08 Product Authenticity

The company sources Goods through legitimate supply channels and supplies them in their original manufacturer packaging. Product specifications, indications and warranties are those of the respective manufacturer; the company acts as a distributor and supplier.

09 Limitation of Liability

To the extent permitted by law, the company's liability in connection with any order is limited to the invoice value of that order. The company is not liable for indirect or consequential loss, or for matters attributable to the manufacturer, the counterparty's handling, or events beyond reasonable control.

10 Governing Law & Jurisdiction

These terms are governed by the laws of India. The courts at Gurugram, Haryana have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute, claim or proceeding arising out of or in connection with these terms or any transaction governed by them.

Refund — 08

Returns & Refunds

Pharmaceutical returns are tightly regulated for patient-safety reasons. The terms below describe the limited circumstances in which the group accepts returns from the trade.

Contents

  1. General Position
  2. Eligible Returns
  3. Notification Window
  4. Conditions for Acceptance
  5. Near-expiry & Expiry
  6. Credit Mechanism
  7. Disputes & Jurisdiction

01 General Position

As a pharmaceutical distributor, the company does not accept returns of correctly supplied Goods on grounds of changed buying preference or over-ordering. Returns are limited to the specific situations described below and are always subject to verification.

02 Eligible Returns

Returns may be considered where: (a) Goods were supplied in error against the confirmed order; (b) Goods were damaged in transit and the damage was recorded on the delivery document; (c) Goods were received with a manufacturing defect; or (d) a product recall has been initiated by the manufacturer or a competent authority.

03 Notification Window

Discrepancies and transit damage under heads (a) and (b) must be reported to the company within three (3) working days of receipt, with supporting evidence and the original delivery document. Suspected manufacturing defects under head (c) should be reported promptly on discovery. Recalls under head (d) follow the timelines stated in the recall notice.

04 Conditions for Acceptance

Goods presented for return must be in their original manufacturer packaging, with intact batch and expiry details, not tampered with, decanted or relabelled, and accompanied by the relevant invoice reference. A return is accepted only after the company issues a return authorisation.

05 Near-expiry & Expiry

Any near-expiry or expiry return is handled strictly in accordance with the policy of the relevant manufacturer and applicable regulation. The company will guide trade partners on the manufacturer's expiry-return window and procedure where such a facility exists.

06 Credit Mechanism

Where a return is approved, the value is generally adjusted as a credit against the counterparty's account or future invoices. Any GST adjustment is processed through a credit note compliant with applicable tax rules. Timelines for manufacturer-linked returns depend on that manufacturer's processing.

07 Disputes & Jurisdiction

Any dispute regarding a return or credit should be raised in writing with the Gurugram office. Unresolved disputes are subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts at Gurugram, Haryana, and governed by Indian law.

Delivery & Dispatch

How orders accepted by Millennium Group are processed, dispatched and delivered to the healthcare trade.

01 Service Area

The group delivers to verified trade addresses across Haryana and the wider National Capital Region where the counterparty holds a valid drug licence and where the group is in a position to serve. Serviceability for a particular location is confirmed at the time of order acceptance.

02 Order Processing

Confirmed trade orders are picked, checked against batch and expiry, and prepared for dispatch as promptly as stock and verification allow. Processing time depends on product availability, order size and successful drug-licence confirmation for new accounts.

03 Dispatch & Carriage

Dispatch is arranged through the group's own delivery arrangements or trusted transport partners selected for pharmaceutical handling. The group aims for dependable, timely delivery, though delivery timelines are good-faith estimates rather than guarantees.

04 Handling & Storage in Transit

Goods are handled to protect product integrity. Where particular products require specific storage conditions, reasonable care is taken in line with the manufacturer's requirements; the counterparty should inspect such Goods promptly on receipt.

05 Delivery & Inspection

On delivery, the counterparty's representative should verify parcel count and check for visible damage, and record any discrepancy on the delivery document before acknowledging receipt. Acceptance without such notation is treated as confirmation of correct delivery for the purposes of the Returns & Refunds policy.

06 Charges

Any delivery or freight charges, where applicable, are advised at the time of order acceptance and itemised on the tax invoice. Minimum-order conditions, if any, are communicated through the office.

07 Failed or Refused Delivery

Where a delivery cannot be completed because the consignee is unavailable, refuses receipt, or the address is unserviceable, the Goods are returned to the company and re-delivery may attract additional charges. Perishable or short-dated Goods are handled in accordance with regulation.

08 Contact

Delivery and dispatch queries are handled by the Gurugram office during working hours; see the Contact page.
